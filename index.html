5 Study Hacks to Boost Your Learning!

Science-backed techniques to enhance your learning efficiency and academic performance

Study Smarter, Not Harder

Are you tired of spending hours studying with little to show for it? Discover these 5 powerful study hacks backed by cognitive science that will revolutionize your learning process and help you achieve better results in less time.

1

The Pomodoro Technique

Break your study time into 25-minute focused intervals followed by 5-minute breaks. After four intervals, take a longer 15-30 minute break.

Pro Tip: Use a timer to enforce the intervals and avoid distractions during the focused periods.
2

Active Recall

Instead of passively rereading notes, actively test yourself on the material. Use flashcards, practice questions, or teach the concept to someone else.

Pro Tip: Create questions for yourself as you study, then answer them later without looking at your notes.
3

Spaced Repetition

Review information at increasing intervals over time. This method strengthens long-term memory by taking advantage of the psychological spacing effect.

Pro Tip: Use apps like Anki or Quizlet that implement spaced repetition algorithms to optimize your review schedule.
4

Interleaving Practice

Instead of studying one topic at a time (blocking), mix different subjects or types of problems during a single study session.

Pro Tip: When practicing math problems, mix algebra, geometry, and calculus questions rather than doing all of one type.
5

The Feynman Technique

Explain a concept in simple terms as if teaching it to a child. Identify gaps in your explanation, then review and simplify further.

Pro Tip: Record yourself explaining a concept, then listen back to identify areas where your explanation was unclear.
